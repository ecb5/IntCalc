A comparably bad choice appears to be \(u=\sin(x)\) so that \(\dx{u}=\cos(x)\dx{x}\) or \(\dx{x}=\frac{\dx{u}}{\cos(x)}\text{.}\) In this case we have
\begin{align} \int\tan(x)\dx{x} = \int\frac{\sin(x)}{\cos(x)}\dx{x}\amp=\int\left(\frac{u}{\cos(x)}\cdot\frac{\dx{u}}{\cos(x)}\right)=\int\frac{u}{1+u^2}\dx{u}\notag\\ \amp=\int\frac{u}{1-u^2}\dx{u}\tag{2.2} \end{align}
which is nearly the same, formally, as equation (2.1). The only difference seems to be that the “\(+\)” in the denominator became a “\(-\text{.}\)”
