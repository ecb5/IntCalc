Consider the following “trick”,
\begin{equation*} \int\sec(x)\dx{x}=\int\sec(x)\frac{\sec(x)+\tan(x){}}{\tan(x)+\sec(x){}}\dx{x}=\int\frac{\sec^2(x)+\sec(x)\tan(x)}{\tan(x)+\sec(x)}\dx{x} \end{equation*}
Do you recognize a substitution that can be used? Use this to integrate \(\sec(x)\dx{x}{}\text{.}\)
