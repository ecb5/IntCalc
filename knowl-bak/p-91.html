In Differential Calculus you saw that while it is not generally true that
\begin{equation} \dfdx{(y\cdot z)}{x}= \dfdx{y}{x}\cdot\dfdx{z}{x}\tag{2.3} \end{equation}
there are certain pairs of functions which are exceptions; for which equation (2.3) is true. Let’s explore this problem in more detail. We will switch to prime notation for aesthetic reasons.
