In Differential Calculus: From Practice to Theory we learned how to differentiate various functions and how to apply this to various modeling problems. For example, one of the exercises had you show that the shape of a hanging chain (the catenary curve) satisfied the differential equation
\begin{equation} \dfdxn{y}{x}{2} =\frac{w}{H}\sqrt{1+\left(\dfdx{y}{x}\right)^2}.\tag{1.1} \end{equation}
