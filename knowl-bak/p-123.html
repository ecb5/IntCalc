Specifically, if we let \(z=\tan{z}{}\text{,}\) then \(\dx{z}=\sec^2(\theta)\dx{\theta}\text{,}\) and so we have
\begin{equation*} \int\frac{1}{\sqrt{1+z^2}}\dx{z}= \int\frac{1} {\sqrt{1+\tan^2(\theta)}}\dx{\theta} = \int\frac{\sec^2(\theta)}{\sec(\theta)}\dx{\theta}=\int\sec(\theta)\dx{\theta}=\ln\abs{\tan(\theta)+\sec(\theta)}+c \end{equation*}
