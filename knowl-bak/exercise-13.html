Problem 1.22.

(a)

If we let \(s\) denote the distance the missile has traveled at time \(t\text{,}\) show that the pursuit curve must satisfy the differential equation

\begin{equation*} \dfdx{y}{x}=\frac{(\frac{s}{k}-y)}{1-x} \end{equation*}

and use this to show that the pursuit curve must satisfy the differential equation

\begin{equation*} (1-x) \dfdxn{y}{x}{2}=1/k \dfdx{s}{x}=1/k \sqrt{1+(\dx{y}/\dx{x})^2} \end{equation*}

with the initial conditions \(y(0)=0\text{,}\) \(\dfdx{y}{x}(0)=0\text{.}\)

(b)

Solve the differential equation in part (a). Hint.
