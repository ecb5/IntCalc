Problem 2.9.

(a)

Use Newton’s Third Law, (\(F\))orce=(\(m\))ass\(\cdot\) (\(a\))cceleration to show that
\begin{equation*} m\dfdx{v}{t}=(m-m_w)g-Rv, \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} v(0)=v_0 \end{equation*}
Let \(M=m-m_w\) and note that \(M\gt0\) (why?). Show that the above leads to the differential equation
\begin{equation*} \frac{\dx{v}}{Rv-Mg}=\frac{-1}{m}\dx{t} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} v(0)=v_0. \end{equation*}

(b)

Use the substitution \(z=Rv-Mg\text{,}\) to rewrite the above equation as
\begin{equation*} \frac{\frac{1}{R}\dx{z}}{z}=-\frac{1}{m}\dx{t} \end{equation*}
and show that
\begin{equation*} v=\frac{Mg}{R}+\left(v_0-\frac{Mg}{R}\right)e^{-\frac{r}{m}t}. \end{equation*}

(c)

Compute \(v\text{.}\) This will be the terminal velocity of the sand settling in the water. Notice that this should be independent of \(v_0\text{.}\) How does your answer for terminal velocity depend on \(M=m-m_w\) and \(R\text{?}\) Does this make sense physically?
