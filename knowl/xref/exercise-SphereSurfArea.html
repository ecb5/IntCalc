Problem 21.5.4.13. The Surface Area of a Sphere.

(a)
Use figure 21.5.4.13 with the equation \(y=\sqrt{a^2-x^2}\text{,}\) \(-a\le x\le a\) to show that the surface area of a sphere of radius \(a\) is \(4\pi a^2\) as Archimedes claimed.
(b)
Use the parameterization
\begin{equation} x=\cos (\theta )\text{,}\tag{21.12} \end{equation}
\begin{equation} y=a\sin( \theta )\text{,}\tag{21.13} \end{equation}
to obtain the same answer as part (a).
