Theorem 21.1.2.5.

The definite integral can be evaluated by computing
\begin{align} \int_{x=a}^{b} f(x)\dx{x}\amp{}= \eval{\int f(x)\dx{x}}{x}{a}^b\notag\\ \amp{} = \eval{\int f(x)\dx{x}}{x}{a}-\eval{\int f(x)\dx{x}}{x}{b}.\tag{21.8} \end{align}
Often we will have a formula, say \(F(x)\text{,}\) for one of the antiderivatives of \(f(x)\) in the most general antiderivative \(\int f(x)\dx{x}\text{.}\) In that case equation (21.8) reduces to
\begin{equation} \int_{x=a}^b f(x)\dx{x} = F(b)-F(a)\tag{21.9} \end{equation}
