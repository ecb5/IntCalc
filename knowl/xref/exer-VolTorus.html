Problem 19.8.3.6. Volume of a torus.

Consider a circle of radius \(b\text{,}\) whose center revolves around a line distance \(a\gt b\) away. The “donut” formed is actually called a torus. Consider the following torus generated by revolving the circle \(x^2+y^2=b^2\) about the line \(x=-a\text{.}\)
Show that the volume of this torus equals the area of the small circle times the circumference of the circle generated by revolving the center of this circle around the line. This was derived by Johannes Kepler 6  (1571- 1630) and is a special case of a theorem by Pappus of Alexandria 7  (290-350 AD).
