Example 14.3.0.4.

Suppose that \(f(x)=\left(\sin(x)+\cos(x)\right)^2\text{.}\) To use the Chain Rule to compute the derivative of \(f(x)\) we need to recognize that \(f(x)\) is the composition of \(\alpha(x)=x^2\text{,}\) and \(\beta(x)=\sin(x)+\cos(x)\) and then apply TheoremÂ 14.3.0.2 as follows.
\begin{align*} f^\prime(x) \amp = \alpha^\prime(\beta(x))\cdot\beta^\prime(x)\\ \amp = \alpha^\prime(\beta(x))\cdot(\cos(x)-\sin(x))\\ \amp = \alpha^\prime(\sin(x)+\cos(x))\cdot(\cos(x)-\sin(x))\\ f^\prime(x)\amp = 2 (\sin(x)+\cos(x))\cdot(\cos(x)-\sin(x)). \end{align*}
