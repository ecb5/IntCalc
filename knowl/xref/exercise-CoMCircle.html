Problem 2.155.

By symmetry, the center of mass of a circle should be the center of the circle, but what about a semicircle? With this in mind, consider the upper semicircle of radius \(r\) centered at the origin. This can be described by the equations
\begin{equation*} x=r\cos( \theta ), y=r\sin (\theta ), 0\le \theta \le \pi \text{.} \end{equation*}
(a)
Verify that \(\bar{x}=0\) by following our process as above.
(b)
Compute \(\bar{y}\text{.}\)
