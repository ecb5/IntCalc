Problem 19.1.0.2. Terminal Velocity.

(a)

Let \(M=m-m_w\) and note that \(M\gt0\text{.}\) (why?) Show that IVP (19.1) can be re-expressed as
\begin{align*} \frac{\dx{v}}{Rv-Mg}=\frac{-1}{m}\dx{t}\amp{}\amp{} v(0)=v_0. \end{align*}

(b)

Use the substitution \(z=Rv-Mg\text{,}\) to rewrite the above equation as
\begin{equation} \frac{\frac{1}{R}\dx{z}}{z}=-\frac{1}{m}\dx{t}.\tag{19.2} \end{equation}

(c)

Integrate both sides of equation (19.2) and use the result to show that
\begin{equation*} v=\frac{Mg}{R}+\left(v_0-\frac{Mg}{R}\right)e^{-\frac{R}{m}t}\text{.} \end{equation*}

(d)

Compute \(\limit{t}{\infty}{v}\text{,}\) and explain why this is called the terminal velocity of the sand settling in the water. Notice that the terminal velocity is independent of \(v_0\text{.}\) Does this make sense physically? Explain.
