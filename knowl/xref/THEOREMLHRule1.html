Theorem 12.4.1.15. L’Hôpital’s Rule, (First Special Case).

Suppose
  1. that \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) are differentiable on an open interval containing \(a\text{,}\)
    🔗
  2. that \(f(a)=g(a)=0\)
    🔗
  3. that \(f^\prime(a)\) and \(g^\prime(a)\) are both defined.
    🔗
  4. and that \(g^\prime(a)\ne0 \text{,}\)
    🔗
Then
\begin{equation*} \limit{x}{a}{\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}}\lhopeq\frac{f^\prime(a)}{g^\prime(a)}\text{.} \end{equation*}
