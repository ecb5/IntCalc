Example 20.2.0.1. Integration By Simple Substitution.

For example, to compute
\begin{equation*} \int\cos(5x) \dx{x}\text{,} \end{equation*}
first make the substitution \(u=5x\text{.}\) In that case \(\dx{u}=5\dx{x}\text{.}\) Substituting these into our integral we have
\begin{equation} \int\cos(5x) \dx{x}= \frac15\int\cos(u) \dx{u}\text{,}\tag{20.8} \end{equation}
and Integration Rule (18.11) tells us that
\begin{equation*} \int\cos(5x) \dx{x} =\frac15\sin(u) +c\text{.} \end{equation*}
Substituting back to the original variable (unsubstituting?) we have
\begin{equation*} \int\cos(5x) \dx{x}= \frac15\sin(5x)+c\text{.} \end{equation*}
