Example 21.1.2.2.

Suppose we are traveling on a straight road at \(60 \frac{miles}{hour}\text{.}\) How far do we travel between the third hour and the eighth hour?
ðŸ”—
Of course you see that if we travel for five hours at \(60 \frac{\text{miles}}{\text{hour}}\) then we travel \(5\times 60 = 300\) miles.
ðŸ”—
In this problem our velocity is \(v(t)=60\text{,}\) \(t_1=3\text{,}\) and \(t_2=8\text{.}\) So the distance traveled will be
\begin{equation*} D= \eval{\int v(t)\dx{t}}{t}{5} - \eval{\int v(t)\dx{t}}{t}{3}\text{.} \end{equation*}
One antiderivative of \(v(t)=60\) is \(P(t)=60t\text{,}\) so
\begin{equation*} D= \eval{60t}{t}{8} - \eval{60t}{t}{3} = 60\cdot8-60\cdot3=300\text{.} \end{equation*}
