Problem 19.14.

(a)

Suppose \(f^\prime(x)\neq{}f(x)\) Show that if
\begin{equation*} \left(f\cdot g\right)^\prime(x) = f^\prime(x)\cdot g^\prime(x) \end{equation*}
then
\begin{equation} g(x)=\exp\left({\int{\frac{1}{1-\frac{f(x)}{f^\prime(x)}}}\dx{x}{}}\right)\tag{19.6} \end{equation}
(Don’t let this formula (19.6) frighten you. Recall that \(\exp(x)=e^x\text{.}\))

(b)

Apply equation (19.6) to the function \(f(x)=x^n\) and compare this to the results in Problem # 60 above.
\begin{equation*} \int{}u\dx{v}{}=\int{} \left(\dx(uv) -v\dx{u}{}\right) = \int\dx(uv) -\int v\dx{u}{=uv-\int v\dx{u} }. \end{equation*}
or
\begin{equation} \int{}u\dx{v}{}=uv-\int v\dx{u}\text{.}\tag{19.7} \end{equation}
