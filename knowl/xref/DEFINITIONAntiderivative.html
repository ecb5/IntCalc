Definition 20.1.2.1. Antiderivative.

Suppose we have a function
\begin{equation} y=F(t)\tag{20.5} \end{equation}
with derivative \(\dfdx{y}{t}=F^\prime(t)=f(t)\text{.}\) Then \(F(t)\) is called an antiderivative of \(f(t)\text{.}\) Since there are infinitely many antiderivatives of a given function we call \(F(t)+C\text{,}\) where \(C\) is an arbitrary constant, the most general antidierivative of \(f(t)\text{.}\)
