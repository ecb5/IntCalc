\(\left.{\frac{y}{x} }\right|_a \RR\) The first section of this book dealt with dealt with Differential Calculus, literally the rules for differences. We learned how to differentiate various functions and how to apply this to various modeling problems. For example, one of the exercises we had was to show that a hanging chain (catenary) satisfied the differential equation
\begin{equation*} \dfdxn{y}{x}{2} =\frac{w}{H}\sqrt{1+\left(\dfdx{y}{x}\right)^2}. \end{equation*}
