Example 19.2.2.1.

Heaviside’s method will not work on the rational functions \(\frac{x^2}{(x-2)(x-3)}\text{.}\) If you try to apply it you’ll get
\begin{equation*} \frac{x^2}{(x-2)(x-3)} = \frac{-4}{x-2}+\frac{9}{x-3} \end{equation*}
which is wrong. Try it and see.
The difficulty here is that the PFD requires that the rational function to be decomposed must be in “lowest terms.” In this context that means that the degree (the value of the largest exponent) of the numerator must be strictly less than the degree of the denominator. In this example \(\deg{(x-2)(x-3)}=\deg{x^2-5x+6}=\deg{x^2}=2\text{,}\) so the PFD doesn’t apply.
This looks at first like it will be a major obstacle but all that is really required is that we do the polynomial division indicated. In this case that means computing
\begin{equation} \frac{x^2}{x^2-5x+6} = 1+ \frac{5x-6}{(x-2)(x-3)}\text{.}\tag{19.42} \end{equation}
From there we can compute the PFD of \(\frac{5x-6}{(x-2)(x-3)}\) as before.
