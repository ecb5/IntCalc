Example 21.4.0.11.

Computing
\begin{equation*} \int_{x=0}^\pi x\sin(x)\dx{x} \end{equation*}
we see that Integration by Parts seems to be the way to go. If we let \(u = x\) and \(\dx{v} = \sin( x) \dx{x} \text{,}\) then
\begin{align*} y \amp =x \amp \dx{v} \amp =\sin(x)\dx{x} \\ \dx{u}\amp =\dx{x} \amp v \amp =-\cos(x) \end{align*}
After applying Theorem 19.3.0.5 we have
\begin{align*} \int_{x=0}^\pi x\sin(x) \dx{x} \amp {}=\ftceval{-x\cos(x)}{x}{0}{\pi} - \int_{x=0}^\pi \cos(x)\dx{x}\\ \amp {}=\left( -\pi \cos(\pi) +0\cos(0)\right)-\ftceval{\sin(x)}{x}{0}{-\pi}\\ \amp =(\pi +0)-(0-0)\\ \amp =\pi. \end{align*}
