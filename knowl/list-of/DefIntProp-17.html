Problem 3.4.0.8.

Show that for the case where \(a\lt{c}\lt{b}\text{,}\) we still have
\begin{equation*} \int_{x=a}^bf\dx{x}+\int_{x=b}^cf\dx{x} = \int_{x=a}^cf\dx{x}. \end{equation*}
Hint.
We already know that
\begin{equation*} \int_{x=a}^bf\dx{x}+\int_{x=a}^cf\dx{x} = \int_{x=c}^bf\dx{x}. \end{equation*}
Solve for \(\int_{x=a}^cf\dx{x}\) and use a previous property.
