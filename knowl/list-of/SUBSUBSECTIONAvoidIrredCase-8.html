Problem 19.2.4.1.

(a)
Let \(i=\sqrt{-1}\) as in Section 8.13 and show that
\begin{equation*} x^2+1 = (x-i)(x+i)\text{.} \end{equation*}
(b)
Use the result of part (a) to show that
\begin{equation*} \int \frac{1}{x^2+1}\dx{x} = \frac{i}{2}\ln \left( \frac{x+i}{x-i} \right) +C \end{equation*}
(c)
Recall that we’ve already seen that \(\int \frac{1}{x^2+1}\dx{x} = \inverse \tan(x) +C\text{.}\) In view of the result in part (b), do you believe that
\begin{equation} \frac{i}{2}\ln \left( \frac{x+i}{x-i} \right) +C = \inverse \tan(x) +C\text{?}\tag{19.54} \end{equation}
Notice that we have not asked if this is true, only if you believe it. Hence there is no right or wrong answer to the following question: Explain why you do or don’t believe that equation (19.54). Give mathematical reasons to support your opinion either way.
