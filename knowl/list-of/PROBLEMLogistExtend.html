Problem 19.2.2.9. An Extension of the Logistic Equation.

As we’ve mentioned before, all mathematical models make subject to simplifying assumptions. It is important to know what are the simplifications are so we know the conditions under which the model is unrealistic. For example, IVP (11.1) works fine in the short term but in the long term it predicts explosive growth which is not sustainable. We addressed this when we generated the Logistic Equation (11.4) by including as a factor, \(\left( 1-\frac{P}{100}\right)\text{.}\) That modification put an upper bound on the population size as we have seen.
However theLogistic Equation doesn’t put any lower bound on the population size. This is also unrealistic since we know that if a population of critters is too small they tend to die out. The following tweak to the logistic model:
\begin{align} \dfdx{P}{t}=0.01P\left(1-\frac{P}{100}\right)\left(\frac{P}{10}-1\right), \amp{}\amp{}P(0)=50.\tag{19.40} \end{align}
forces a population which is to small to die out.
(a)
In this case, \(10\) is called the minimum viability level of the population. Use the graphical techniques of Section 8.6 to explain what happens if our population drops below this value.
(b)
Of course it would be better to have a formula for \(P(t)\text{,}\) and it appears that we obtain one simply by integrating both sides of equation (19.40). Can we? Try it and see what happens.
