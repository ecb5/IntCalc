Problem 2.2.3.14.
Of course, we’re not just computing the PFDs for fun. For us the Partial Fraction Decomposition is a tool which will enable us compute integrals we can’t compute by other means. Notice that we cannot compute any of the integrals in this problem without first using the PFD to change the integrand into something more manageable.
(a)
Combine your results from part (a) of Problem 2.2.3.9 and what you learned from Example 2.2.3.10 to compute
\begin{equation*} \int \frac{1}{x^2(x-1)(x-3)^3}\dx{x} \end{equation*}
(b)
Combine your results from part (c) of Problem 2.2.3.9 and what you learned from Example 2.2.3.10 to compute
\begin{equation*} \int\frac{x-2}{(x+1)^3}\dx{x}\text{.} \end{equation*}
