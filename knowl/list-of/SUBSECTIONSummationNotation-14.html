Problem 22.2.2.8.

Earlier, we obtained
\begin{equation*} \int^{\frac{4}{3}}_{z=\frac{2}{3}}{e^{-{\frac{1}{2}z}^2}\dx{z}}=\left(\frac{4}{3}-\frac{2}{3}\right)-\frac{1}{1!\cdot 2\cdot 3}\left({\left(\frac{4}{3}\right)}^3-{\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)}^3\right)+\frac{1}{2!\cdot 2^2\cdot 5}\left({\left(\frac{4}{3}\right)}^5-{\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)}^5\right)-\frac{1}{3!\cdot 2^3\cdot 7}\left({\left(\frac{4}{3}\right)}^7-{\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)}^7\right)+\frac{1}{4!\cdot 2^4\cdot 9}\left({\left(\frac{4}{3}\right)}^9-{\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)}^9\right)-\dots \end{equation*}
by integrating the power series
\begin{equation*} e^{-\frac{1}{2}z^2}=1-\frac{z^2}{1!\cdot 2}+\frac{z^4}{2!\cdot 2^2}-\frac{z^6}{3!\cdot 2^3}+\frac{z^8}{4!\cdot 2^4}-\dots \end{equation*}
term by term.
(a)
Write this final answer in summation notation.
(b)
