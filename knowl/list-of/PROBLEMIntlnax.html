Problem 19.1.2.3.

(a)
Verify that the identity \(\int\ln(x)\dx{x} = x\ln(x) -x + C\) is correct by differentiating.
(b)
Recall that \(\ln(ab)=\ln(a)+\ln(b)\text{.}\) Use this and the result of part (a) to compute the value of
\begin{equation*} \int \ln(ax)\dx{x}\text{.} \end{equation*}
(c)
Now use the formula from part (a) and the substitution \(u=ax\) to compute
\begin{equation*} \int \ln(ax)\dx{x} \end{equation*}
where \(a\) is an arbitrary constant. Was your result the same as in part (b)?
(d)
Differentiate the function you found in part (c) to confirm that your solution is correct.
