DIGRESSION: How Not to Integrate a Product.

In the beginning a common error is to assume that that we need only integrate the two factors separately. If we do that with formula (2.63) we’ll get
\begin{equation*} \frac{x^2}{2}\sin(x) +C\text{.} \end{equation*}
This is not correct as we can easily verify by differentiation. Moreover, we know that the differential of a product is not the product of the differentials, so on second thought that may seem like it was a silly idea. It was not. It is the most natural assumption we can make. But it is an assumption, not knowledge, and when it is examined closely it comes up short as we have just seen.
So we need a new idea.
