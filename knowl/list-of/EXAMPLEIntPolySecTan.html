Example 19.1.2.16.
(a)
For our first example we will compute
\begin{align*} \int\sec^3(x)\dx{x} \amp{}= \int \sec^2(x)\sec{x}\dx{x} \end{align*}
From equation (19.11) we have
\begin{align*} \int\sec^3(x)\dx{x}\amp{}=\frac14\int(u+v)^2\sec(x)\dx{x}\\ \amp{}=\frac14\int(u^2+2uv+v^2)\sec(x)\dx{x} \end{align*}
and from equation (19.13) we have
\begin{align*} \int\sec^3(x)\dx{x}\amp{}=\frac14\int(u^2+2+v^2))\sec(x)\dx{x}\\ =\amp{}\frac14\int u^2\left(\frac{1}{u}\dx{u}\right) -\frac14 \int v^2\left(\frac{1}{v}\dx{v}\right)+\frac12\int\sec(x)\dx{x}\\ =\amp{}\frac18\left(u^2-v^2\right)+\frac12\int\sec(x)\dx{x}\\ =\amp{}\frac18\left[\sec(x)+\tan(x)\right]^2 -\frac18\left[\sec(x)-\tan(x)\right]^2 +\frac12\abs{\sec(x)+\tan(x)}+C\\ =\amp{}\frac18\left[\sec^2(x)+2\sec(x)\tan(x) +\tan^2(x)\right]\\ \amp{}-\frac18\left[\sec^2(x)-2\sec(x)\tan(x) +\tan^2(x)\right]\\ \amp{}+\frac12\abs{\sec(x)+\tan(x)}+C. \end{align*}
So, finally,
\begin{equation*} \int\sec^3(x)\dx{x}=\frac12\left(\sec(x)\tan(x)+\abs{\sec(x)+\tan(x)}\right)+C. \end{equation*}
