Aside: Mathematical Terminology.

Technically speaking, a statement is not a theorem until it has actually been proved. Until then it is a conjecture.

The distinction between a theorem and a lemma is very slight and rather arbitrary. Typically we call a statement a theorem if it is important and requires proof. We call it a lemma if it requires proof itself, and is used to simplify the proof of a theorem. This is not a hard-and-fast rule by any means. Sometimes we first prove a lemma as an aid to proving a theorem, only to find that the lemma is actually more important. However, having been originally dubbed a lemma the result is known ever after as a lemma.
