Problem 19.1.3.2.

Returning to the Example 18.1.0.11 we see that it was originally given in terms of \(y\) where \(y(x)\) represents the vertical position of the Hanging Chain. We had made the substitution \(z=\dfdx{y}{x}\) to make the original equation a little “easier on the eyes.”
So we are interested in finding \(y\) not \(z\text{.}\)
For convenience we take the lowest point to be the origin of our coordinate system so that at the lowest point \((x,y)=(0,0)\text{.}\)
Observe that at the lowest point of the curve the catenary will be horizontal so its slope will \(\eval{\dfdx{y}{x}}{x}{0}=0\text{.}\)
(a)
Show that our problem can be re–cast as the IVP
\begin{align*} \dfdx{y}{x}\amp{}=z\amp{}z(0)=0. \end{align*}
(b)
Integrate both sides of the differential equation to show that
\begin{equation*} \ln\abs{z+ \sqrt{1+z^2}} = \frac{W}{H}x +C\text{.} \end{equation*}
(c)
Show that \(c=0\) and so
\begin{equation*} \dfdx{y}{x}=z=\frac12\left(e^{\frac{w}{H}x}-e^{-\frac{w}{H}x}\right). \end{equation*}
Note to self.
The use of \(y\) and \(z\) interchangeably is confusing.
(d)
Integrate the result in part (a) to obtain
\begin{equation*} y=\frac{H}{w}\left(\frac{e^{\frac{wx}{H}}+e^{\frac{-wx}{H}}}{2}\right)+c \end{equation*}
which is the equation of the catenary as stated earlier.
