Problem 1.1.0.10. Find the pattern.

(a)

Solve the differential equation
\begin{equation*} y^{\prime} +2y =0 \end{equation*}
(This is a first order differential equation because the highest order derivative that appears in it is the first.)

(b)

Solve the differential equation
\begin{equation*} y^{\prime\prime} +2y^\prime =0 \end{equation*}
(This is a second order differential equation because the highest order derivative that appears in it is the second.)
Hint 1.
Make it “easier on your eyes.”
Hint 2.
Make the substitution: \(z=y^\prime \text{.}\)

(c)

Solve the differential equation
\begin{equation*} 3y^{\prime\prime\prime} -5y^{\prime\prime} =0 \end{equation*}
What is the order of this differential equation?
