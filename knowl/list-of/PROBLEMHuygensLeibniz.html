Problem 21.2.2.5. Sum of the Reciprocals of the Triangular Numbers.

Back when we were talking about Leibniz and the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, we mentioned that Huygens gave Leibniz the problem of summing the reciprocals of a all of the triangular numbers. That is, he had to sum
\begin{equation} \frac{1}{1}+\frac{1}{1+2}+\frac{1}{1+2+3}+\dots \tag{21.1} \end{equation}
(a)
Use summation notation to express formula (21.1).
(b)
The numbers \(T_n=1+2+3+\dots +n\) is called the \(n\text{th}\) triangular number because they represent the number of dots arranged in a triangular pattern.
(c)
We also mentioned the formula
\begin{equation*} T_n=\frac{n\left(n+1\right)}{2} \end{equation*}
There are different ways, both geometric and algebraic, to show this formula. We will use summation notation to obtain it.
Show that
\begin{equation*} \sum^n_{j=1}{j}=T_n=\sum^n_{j=1}{(n+1-j)} \end{equation*}
and use this to show that
\begin{equation*} 2T_n=\sum^n_{j=1}{(n+1)} \end{equation*}
to obtain the formula for \(T_n\text{.}\)
