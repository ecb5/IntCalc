Problem 19.1.4.11.

A surprising number of integrals involve terms such as \(\sqrt{1-x^2}\text{,}\) \(\sqrt{1+x^2}\) or \(\sqrt{x^2-1}\text{.}\) When they occurr it is often advantageous to use a trigonometric substitution to remove the square root, which is typically a bigger concern than the trigonometric functions. However, you should remember that there are other techniques at your disposal. And frequently, when you have a choice of techniques one will be simpler than the other.
