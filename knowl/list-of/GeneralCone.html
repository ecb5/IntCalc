Problem 3.5.2.13. The Generalized Cone.

Consider the following “generalized cone.”
Show that the volume of this cone is \(1/3\) the volume of the “generalized cylinder” with the same height and same base. That is, show that the volume of this cone is \(3 \text{ (Area of base)}\times \text{ height }\text{.}\)
Hint.
The generic slice is similar to the base. When you set up a proportion between the areas, it is proportional to the squares of the distances from the apex.
