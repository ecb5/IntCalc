Problem 19.2.2.6. The PFD in its Simplest Form (Find the Pattern).

We’ve arrived at a very clever algorithm for computing the PFD of a rational function of the form \(\frac{c}{(x-a)(x-b)}\) but we’ll need to extend it considerably if it is going to be more than minimally useful. This extension is straightforward.
(a)
Show that the PFD of \(\frac{1}{(x-a)(x-b)(x-c)}\) can be computed in exactly the same manner as our most recent method for \(\frac{1}{(x-a)(x-b)}\text{.}\)
(b)
Show the same for \(\frac{1}{(x-a)(x-b)(x-c)(x-d)}\text{.}\)
(c)
Show the same for \(\frac{1}{(x-x_1)(x-x_2)(x-x_3)\cdots(x-x_n)}\text{.}\)
