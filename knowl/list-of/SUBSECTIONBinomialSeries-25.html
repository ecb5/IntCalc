Problem 5.2.5.4.

In most books, you will see the binomial theorem written as
\begin{equation*} {\left(a+b\right)}^N=a^Nb^0+\frac{N}{1!}a^{N-1}b^1+\frac{N\left(N-1\right)}{2!}a^{N-2}b^2+\frac{N\left(N-1\right)\left(N-2\right)}{3!}{a^{N-3}b}^3+\dots +\frac{N\left(N-1\right)\left(N-2\right)\left(N-3\right)\cdot \dots \cdot 2\cdot 1}{N!}{a^0b}^N \end{equation*}
You can see that our formulation is a special case of this where \(a=1\) and \(b=x\text{.}\) It turns out that our formulation can be used to obtain the general formula as well, so they are logically equivalent. To see this, rewrite \({\left(a+b\right)}^N=a^N{\left(1+\frac{b}{a}\right)}^N\text{.}\) Let \(x=\frac{b}{a}\) in
\begin{equation*} {\left(1+x\right)}^N=1+\frac{N}{1!}x+\frac{N\left(N-1\right)}{2!}x^2+\frac{N\left(N-1\right)\left(N-2\right)}{3!}x^3+\dots +\frac{N\left(N-1\right)\left(N-2\right)\left(N-3\right)\cdot \dots \cdot 2\cdot 1}{N!}x^N \end{equation*}
to obtain the general formula
\begin{equation*} {\left(a+b\right)}^N=a^Nb^0+\frac{N}{1!}a^{N-1}b^1+\frac{N\left(N-1\right)}{2!}a^{N-2}b^2+\frac{N\left(N-1\right)\left(N-2\right)}{3!}{a^{N-3}b}^3+\dots +\frac{N\left(N-1\right)\left(N-2\right)\left(N-3\right)\cdot \dots \cdot 2\cdot 1}{N!}{a^0b}^N \end{equation*}
