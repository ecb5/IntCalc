Problem 19.2.3.15.
(a)
Apply Theorem 19.2.3.7 three times to show that the following PFD is valid
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{\left(x-a\right)\left(x-b\right)\left(x-c\right)}=\frac{A}{x-a}+\frac{B}{x-b}+\frac{C}{x-c} \end{equation*}
where \(a,b,c\ \)are distinct numbers
(b)
Use Theorem 19.2.3.7 to show that the following PFD is also valid
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{\left(x-a\right)\left(x-b\right)\left(x-c\right)\left(x-d\right)}=\frac{A}{x-a}+\frac{B}{x-b}+\frac{C}{x-c}+\frac{D}{x-d}\ \end{equation*}
where \(a,b,c,d\) are distinct numbers.
