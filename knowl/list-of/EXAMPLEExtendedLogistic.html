Example 19.2.1.9.
As we’ve mentioned before, all mathematical models make simplifying assumptions. It is important to know what are the simplifications are so we know the conditions under which the model is unrealistic. For example, IVP (11.1) works fine in the short term but in the long term it predicts explosive growth which is not sustainable. We addressed this when we generated the Logistic Equation (11.4) by including as a factor, \(\left( 1-\frac{P}{100}\right)\text{.}\) That modification put an upper bound on the population size as we have seen.
However the Logistic Equation doesn’t put any lower bound on the population size. This is also unrealistic since we know that if a population of critters is too small they tend to die out. The following tweak to the logistic model:
\begin{equation} \dfdx{P}{t}=0.01P\left(1-\frac{P}{100}\right)\left(\frac{P}{10}-1\right),\tag{19.41} \end{equation}
forces a population which is too small to die out. In this case, \(10\) is called the minimum viability level of the population.
