Problem 2.3.0.11.

To avoid obscuring our point, so far we’ve kept the numerators and the coefficients of \(x\) simple. The numerators have always been some constant, usually \(1\text{,}\) and the coefficients of \(x\) have always been \(1\text{.}\) But this is not necessary.
Use Heaviside’s method to compute each of the following integrals. Once you have the decomposition confirm that it is correct by adding your fractions together.

(a)

\(\displaystyle\int \frac{x}{(x^2-4 )(16x^2- 9)}\dx{x}\)

(b)

\(\displaystyle\int \frac{x^2-1}{(3x-1 )(x-2 )(x-3 )}\dx{x}\)

(c)

\(\displaystyle\int \frac{(x-1)(x-2)}{(2x+1 )(x+2)(3-x)}\dx{x}\)

(d)

\(\displaystyle\int \frac{x^3}{x(x^2-1)(x-3)(5x+8)}\dx{x}\)

(e)

\(\displaystyle\int \frac{x(x^3-1)}{(x-2 )(x+7 )(x-5 )(x+8)(x-3)}\dx{x}\)
