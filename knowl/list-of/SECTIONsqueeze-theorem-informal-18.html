\(\limit{x}{\infty}{\frac{x\cos(x)}{x^2+1}}\)
🔗
🔗

(b)

\(\limit{x}{-\infty}{\frac{x\cos(x)}{x^2+1}}\)
🔗
🔗

(c)

\(\limit{x}{\infty}{\frac{\lfloor x\rfloor}{x^2}},\) where \(\lfloor x\rfloor\) represents the greatest integer less than or equal to \(x\text{.}\)
🔗
Aside: Mathematical Notation.
\(\lfloor x\rfloor\) is called the “greatest integer” function in mathematics, and the “floor” function in computer science. It rounds down to the greatest integer less than \(x\text{.}\) For example \(\lfloor5.7\rfloor =5\text{,}\) \(\lfloor2\rfloor=2\text{,}\) \(\lfloor\pi\rfloor=3\text{,}\) etc.
