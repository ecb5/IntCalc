Problem 19.1.4.3.

Of course, the tangent function is not the only trigonometric function at our disposal. The following is the view from above of a tractor–trailer. Initially, the center of the front axle of the tractor is at the origin and the center of the rear axle is at the point \((1,0)\text{.}\)
Suppose the tractor pulls the front wheels upward along the \(y\)–axis (the line \(x=0\)) and that the rear wheels don’t slip.
(a)
Show the path that the center of the rear axle of the trailer follows must satisfy the IVP
\begin{align*} \dfdx{y}{x}=\frac{-\sqrt{1-x^2}}{x}, \amp{}\amp{} y(1)=0 \end{align*}
(b)
Find the equation, \(y=y(x)\) of the tractrix.
Hint.
After you separate the variables make the substitution \(x=\sin(\theta)\) and impose the condition \(0\lt \theta \lt \frac{\pi }{2}\text{.}\) This ensures that
\begin{equation*} \sqrt{1-\sin^2( \theta )}=\abs{\cos (\theta )}=\cos (\theta ) \end{equation*}
because it forces \(0\lt x\lt 1\text{.}\)
(c)
Plot your solution on the \(x-y\) plane.
