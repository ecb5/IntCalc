Problem 19.2.3.6.
Let’s re-examine the PFD for \(\frac{x^2-x+3}{x^2\left(x-1\right)}\) from part (b) in the last problem. We could have directly noticed that the form of our PFD is
\begin{equation*} \frac{x^2-x+3}{x^2\left(x-1\right)}=\frac{A}{x}+\frac{B}{x^2}+\frac{C}{x-1} \end{equation*}
Use the HCUM directly on this to compute \(B=3,\ C=-1\text{.}\) Employ any technique you wish to determine that \(A=2\) and compare this to the answer you obtained in part (b) of the last problem.
