Task [STRUCT].10.b.
The integrals in Problem [STRUCT].10.a and Problem [STRUCT].10.b can be evaluated by means of several different substitutions as we’ve seen. There are others that will also work but our principle point for now is that no substitution is “wrong”, some result in a new integral that is easier than the original and some will not. If our goal is simply to evaluate the integral we’ll want to look for the simplest method we can find.
(a)
Find a simpler substitution than the one we used in Problem [STRUCT].10.a to evaluate the integral
\begin{equation*} \int\frac{x}{1+x^2}\dx{x} \end{equation*}
Hint.
Notice that \(x\dx{x}\) is almost the differential of \(1+x^2\text{.}\)
(b)
Find a simpler substitution than the one we used in Problem [STRUCT].10.b to evaluate the integral
\begin{equation*} \int\frac{x}{1-x^2}\dx{x} \end{equation*}
Hint.
The substitution you used in part [STRUCT].10.a will probably work here.
