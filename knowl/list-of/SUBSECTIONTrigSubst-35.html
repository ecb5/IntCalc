Example 19.1.4.25.

If tangent occurs to an odd power, we can save a tangent and a secant as part of the differential and use the same identity to convert everything else to secant.
\begin{equation*} \int\sec^2(\theta)\tan^3(\theta)\dx{\theta} = \int\sec^2(\theta)\tan^2(\theta)\sec(\theta)\tan(\theta)\dx{\theta{}}=\int\sec^2(\theta)(\sec^2(\theta)-1)\sec(\theta)\tan(\theta)\dx{\theta}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Letting \(u=\sec(\theta)\) we have
\begin{equation*} \int u^2(u^2-1)\dx{u}=\int (u^4-u^2)\dx{u}=\frac{u^5}{5}-\frac{u^3}{3}+c=\frac{\sec^5(\theta)}{5}-\frac{\sec^3(\theta)}{3}+c\text{.} \end{equation*}
