Problem 2.2.2.6.

(a)
Choose appropriate values of \(x\) to substitute into
\begin{equation*} 1=A\left(x-b\right)\left(x-c\right)+B\left(x-a\right)\left(x-c\right)+C(x-a)(x-b) \end{equation*}
to determine that
\begin{align*} A\amp{}=\frac{1}{\left(a-b\right)\left(a-c\right)}\\ B\amp{}=\frac{1}{\left(b-a\right)\left(b-c\right)}\\ C \amp{}=\frac{1}{\left(c-a\right)\left(c-b\right)} \end{align*}
Again, you should be able to do this with minimal calculation.
(b)
Use the above result to compute
\begin{equation*} \int{\frac{1}{\left(x-a\right)\left(x-b\right)\left(x-c\right)}\ \dx{x}} \end{equation*}
(c)
Compute
\begin{equation*} \int\frac{1}{\left(x-a\right)\left(x-b\right)\left(x-c\right)\left(x-d\right)} \dx{x} \end{equation*}
and use this to compute \(\int\frac{1}{x^4-13x^2+36}\dx{x} \) directly and compared this to what you obtained in the last problem.
