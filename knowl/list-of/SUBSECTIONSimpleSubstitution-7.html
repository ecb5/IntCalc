Problem 2.1.2.3.

(a)
Compute the integral
\begin{equation*} \int (x-1)(x-2)\dx{x}\text{.} \end{equation*}
by multiplying out the integrand and then integrating.
(b)
Compute the integral
\begin{equation*} \int (x-1)(x-2)\dx{x}\text{.} \end{equation*}
using the substitution \(u=x-1\text{.}\)
(c)
Compute the integral
\begin{equation*} \int (x-1)(x-2)\dx{x}\text{.} \end{equation*}
using the substitution \(u=x-2\text{.}\)
(d)
Show that the answers you obtained in parts (a), (b), and (c) are all equivalent.
