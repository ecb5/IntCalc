Problem 4.5.5.10. Pappus’ Theorem for Surface Area.

Consider the following curve with center of mass \(\bar{X} =(\bar{x}, \bar{y} )\) revolved about a vertical line \(x=c\) to the left of the curve.
Show that the surface area of the surface generated by revolving this curve about the line is given by
\begin{equation*} 2\pi (\bar{x} -c)\int_{X=A}^B \dx{s} = (\text{circumference of circle with radius } \bar{x} -c)\cdot (\text{length of curve}) \end{equation*}
