Problem 19.3.0.13.

Suppose \(n\) is a positive integer. We want to compute the integral
\begin{equation*} \int \sin^n(x) \dx{x}\text{.} \end{equation*}

(a)

First let \(u=\sin^{n-1}\) and \(\dx{v}=\sin (x)\dx{x}\) and show that
\begin{equation*} \int \sin^n (x)\dx{x} = -\frac1n \cos(x) \sin^{n-1} (x)+(n-1)\int \sin^{n-2}(x)\cos^2(x) \dx{x}\text{.} \end{equation*}

(b)

Show that
\begin{equation*} \int \sin^n (x)\dx{x} = -\frac1n \cos(x) \sin^{n-1} (x)+(n-1)\int \sin^{n-2}(x) \dx{x}-(n-1)\int \sin^n(x) \dx{x}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Hint.
Observe that \(\cos^2(x) =1-\sin^2(x) \text{.}\)

(c)

Explain how the result in part (b) implies that
\begin{equation} \int \sin^n (x)\dx{x} = -\frac1n \cos(x) \sin^{n-1} (x)+ \frac{n-1}{n}\int \sin^{n-2}(x) \dx{x}\text{.}\tag{19.63} \end{equation}
