Problem 21.2.8.4.

\begin{equation*} \frac{d^2y}{dx^2}=x^2y \end{equation*}

\begin{equation*} y=a_0\left(1+\frac{1}{3\cdot 4}x^4+\frac{1}{\left(3\cdot 4\right)\left(7\cdot 8\right)}x^8+\frac{1}{\left(3\cdot 4\right)\left(7\cdot 8\right)\left(11\cdot 12\right)}x^{12}+\dots \right)+a_1\left(x+\frac{1}{4\cdot 5}x^5+\frac{1}{\left(4\cdot 5\right)\left(8\cdot 9\right)}x^9+\frac{1}{\left(4\cdot 5\right)\left(8\cdot 9\right)\left(12\cdot 13\right)}x^{13}+\dots \right) \end{equation*}
for arbitrary constants \(a_0\) and \(a_1\text{.}\) What would the next term in each sum look like?
