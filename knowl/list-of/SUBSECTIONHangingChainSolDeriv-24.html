Problem 2.1.3.3.

Let’s assume that the lowest point on the chain is at \((0,0)\text{.}\)
(a)
Use equation (2.19) to show that \(z\left(0\right)=0\) so that \(C=0\text{.}\)
(b)
Show that z must satisfy
\begin{equation*} \sqrt{1+z^2}=e^{\frac{w}{H}x}-z \end{equation*}
(c)
Square both sides to show that \(z\) must satisfy
\begin{equation*} 2e^{\frac{w}{H}x}z={\left(e^{\frac{w}{H}x}\right)}^2-1 \end{equation*}
\end{enumerate} and ultimately
\begin{equation*} z=\frac{1}{2}\left(e^{\frac{w}{H}x}-e^{-\frac{w}{H}x}\right) \end{equation*}
