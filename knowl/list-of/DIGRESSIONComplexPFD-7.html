Example 19.2.2.12.
By allowing the use of complex numbers we can use the HCUM to compute
\begin{align*} \frac{x-2}{x(x^2+1)}=\frac{x-2}{x(x-i)(x+i)} \amp{}=\frac{-2}{x}+\frac{\frac{i-2}{i(2i)}}{x-i}+\frac{\frac{i+2}{i(-2i)}}{x+i}\\ \amp{}= \frac{-2}{x}+\frac{\frac{i-2}{-2}}{x-i}+\frac{\frac{-i-2}{-2}}{x+i}\\ \amp{}=\frac{-2}{x}+\frac{1-\frac12i}{x-i}+\frac{1+\frac12i }{x+i}. \end{align*}
But unfortunately, in this course we confine our attention to the real numbers and treat the complex numbers as if they are somehow unsavory, even when using them would make more sense. To get the decomposition back into just the real number we compute the sum \(\frac{1-\frac12i}{x-i}+\frac{1+\frac12i }{x+1}\) as follows
\begin{equation} \frac{1-\frac12i}{x-i}+\frac{1+\frac12i }{x+i}=\frac{2x+1}{x^2+1}\text{.}\tag{19.52} \end{equation}
Thus our decompostion over the real numbers (rather than the complex numbers is
\begin{equation} \frac{x-2}{x(x^2+1)}=\frac{-2}{x}+\frac{2x+1}{x^2+1}\text{.}\tag{19.53} \end{equation}
