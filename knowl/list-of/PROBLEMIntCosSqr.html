Problem 2.1.4.7.

Show that
\begin{equation*} \int \cos^2 (\theta )\dx{\theta }=\frac12 \theta +\frac14 \sin (2\theta )+C \end{equation*}
as we stated in Example 2.1.4.5.
Hint.
Recall the Half–Angle formula from Trigonometry:
\begin{equation*} \cos^2(x) = \frac12 \left( 1+\cos(2x)\right) \end{equation*}
