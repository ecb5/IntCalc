Problem 19.2.3.1.

(a)
Use the Heaviside’s Cover–up method to find \(B\) and \(C\) in the decomposition
\begin{equation*} \frac{x}{x^2(x-1)} = \frac{A}{x} + \frac{B}{x^2}+ \frac{C}{x-1}\text{.} \end{equation*}
(b)
The Cover–up method won’t find \(A\) for us (yet). Find \(A\) by any method you can.
