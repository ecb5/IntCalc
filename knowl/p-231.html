As before, we want to find coefficients \(a_0, a_1,\) and \(a_2\) so that
\begin{gather*} 1+2x+x^2 = a_0 + a_1(x-1) + a_2(x-1)^2. \end{gather*}
Dividing both sides by \(x-1\) we get \begin{description} \item[\sc{}On the right:] a_1+a_2(x-1)$ with the remainder $a_0.$ \item[\sc{}On the left:] $x+3$ with the remainder $4.$ \end{description} So $a_0=4$ and $x+3=a_1+a_2(x-1).$ Dividing again by $x-1$ gives \begin{description} \item[\sc{}On the right:] $a_2$ with the remainder $a_1.$ \item[\sc{}On the left:] $1$ with the remainder $4.$ \end{description} Therefore $$ 1+2x+x^2 = 4+4(x-1)+(x-1)^2. $$ \end{myexample}
