A later exercise had you show that
\begin{equation*} y=\frac{H}{w}\left(\frac{e^{\frac{wx}{H}}+e^{\frac{-wx}{H}}}{2}\right) \end{equation*}
in fact, satisfied this differential equation. All this is well and good, but you might have been wondering at the time where we pulled this function out of to differentiate. This is what integral calculus is about. In general, given a function \(y=y(x)\text{,}\) we can differentiate it to obtain the differential . Integration is the process of starting with a differential and finding the function that was differentiated to obtain it.
