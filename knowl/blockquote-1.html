Quotation

I cannot but see a stark contradiction between the intuitively clear fundamental formulas of the integral calculus and the incomparably artificial and complex work of their "justification" and their "proofs". One must be quite stupid not to see this at once, and quite careless if, after having seen this, one can get used to this artificial, logical atmosphere, and can later on forget this stark contradiction.
-- Nikolai Nikolaievich Luzin 2 , (1883-1950)
