When we consider \(\int_{x=a}^by\dx{x}\) with \(a\lt b\text{,}\) then the difference \(\dx{x}\) is a positive change. When we consider \(\int_{x=a}^b y\dx{x}\text{,}\) then the difference 𝑑𝑥 represents a negative change. Hence, we have
\begin{equation*} \int_{x=b}^ay\dx{x}=-\int_{x=a}^by\dx{x}. \end{equation*}
