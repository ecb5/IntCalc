Since sine is raised to an odd power, it makes sense to “save one of the sines” and convert everything else to cosine, utilizing the Pythagorean identity \(\sin^2(\theta)=1-\cos^2(\theta)\text{,}\) and then apply a substitution as follows. First we "save a sine."
\begin{equation*} \int\sin^3(\theta)\cos^2(\theta)\dx{\theta} = \int\sin(\theta)\sin^2(\theta)\cos^2(\theta)\dx{\theta}=\int(1-\cos^2(\theta))\cos^2(\theta)\sin(\theta)\dx{\theta}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Next we let \(z=\cos(\theta)\text{,}\) so that \(\dx{z}=-\sin(\theta)\dx{\theta}\text{,}\) which gives
\begin{align*} \int\sin^3(\theta)\cos^2(\theta)\dx{\theta} \amp= \int(1-z^2)z^2(-\dx{z})\\ \amp=\int(z^4-z^2)\dx{z}\\ \amp=\frac{z^5}{5}=\frac{z^3}{3}+c\\ \amp=\frac{\cos^5(\theta)}{5}=\frac{\cos^3(\theta)}{3}+c\text{.} \end{align*}
