We saw some examples of Differential Equations when we studied differentiation. For example, we saw in [cross-reference to target(s) "SECTIONGalileoDrops" missing or not unique] that Galileo found empirically that a body falling freely near the surface of the earth would accelerate downward at a constant rate we’ve called \(g\text{.}\) And Newton’s Second Law of Motion states that if we have a body with mass equal to one kilogram (\(m=1\)) falling freely under the force of the earth’s gravitational attraction then its acceleration, \(a\) is the derivative of its velocity, \(v\text{.}\) So: \(a=\dfdx{v}{t}\text{.}\) This is fully described by the differential equation
\begin{equation} \dfdx{v}{t}=g,\tag{2.9} \end{equation}
which is a simple model of the motion of a falling body.
