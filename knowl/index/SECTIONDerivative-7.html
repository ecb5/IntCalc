In his 1797 work Théorie des Fonctions Analytiques 20  (The Theory of Analytic Functions), Joseph Louis Lagrange 21  (1736–1813) attempted to make Calculus more rigorous. He even coined a new term for the differential ratio. He called it the fonction d\’{eriv\’{e}e} (meaning a function derived from another function). He also replaced the differential ratio \(\dfdx{y}{x}\) with the more modern function notation \(y^\prime(x)\) (read this aloud as “\(y\) prime of \(x\)”).
