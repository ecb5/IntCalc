A good Rule of Thumb 1  for choosing a substitution is to look a the integrand (the part between \(\int \) and the differential) and see if you can find an expression and its derivative. That is how we chose the substitution \(z=\cos(x) \) in Example 3.1.0.6, and what the hints in Problem 3.1.0.10 tried to lead you to. If you can’t find an expression and its derivative try to find an expression and almost its derivative.
