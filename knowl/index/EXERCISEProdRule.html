Problem 22.2.0.1.

(a)

Show that if we let \(\dx{v}=v+a\) in the integration by parts formula, where \(a\) is any constant, then we will obtain the same result as before, namely \(\int{u\dx{v}}=uv-\int{v\dx{u}}\text{.}\)

(b)

Use integration by parts with \(u=x^2\) and \(\dx{v}=e^x\dx{x}\) to obtain
\begin{equation*} \int{x^2e^x}\dx{x}=x^2e^x-\int{2xe^x \dx{x}} \end{equation*}

(c)

Use integration by parts on \(\int{2xe^x\dx{x}}\) with \(u=e^x\) and \(\dx{v}=2x\dx{x}\) and substitute this into the result in part (a). What happened? This is not a fluke! It will happen every time if you switch the roles of \(u\) and \(v\text{.}\)

(d)

Perform integration by parts again on the result in part a to obtain a final answer for \(\int{x^2e^x}\dx{x}\text{.}\)
