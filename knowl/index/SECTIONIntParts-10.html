Problem 20.5.0.4.

Notice that in Integration Rule (18.10) shows us how to integrate the natural exponential function but we have no analog for the integral of \(\ln(x)\text{.}\) Perform Integration By Parts on
\begin{equation*} \int \ln(x) \end{equation*}
using \(u=\ln(x){}\) and \(\dx{v}=\dx{x}\) to compute the integral.

A Word of Advice.

Rather than trying to memorize this integral, just remember to use integration by parts.
