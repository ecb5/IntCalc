Drill 2.1.2.2.

Integration by substitution seems to be a fairly simple technique. And it is simple, at least in concept. But in fact substitution is a very sophisticated idea which is used at all levels of mathematics as you’ve already seen. Nearly every integral you do from now on will involve some sort of a substitution at some point in the computation. You need to get as much practice with it as you can.
Compute each of these integrals by making the indicated substitution. Assume \(a\) is a constant.
  1. \(\displaystyle\int \sin(2x)\dx{x}\text{,}\)
    Substitution: \(u=ax\)
  2. \(\displaystyle\int 2x(5x-2)^3\dx{x}\text{,}\)
    Substitution: \(u=5x-2\)
  3. \(\displaystyle\int 2x\sqrt{5x-2}\dx{x}\text{,}\)
    Substitution: \(u=5x-2\)
  4. \(\displaystyle \int x^2\left(5x^3-2\right) \dx{x}\)
    Substitution: \(u=5x^3-2\)
  5. \(\displaystyle\int \sec(ax)\tan(ax)\dx{x}\text{,}\)
    Substitution: \(u=ax\)
  6. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{1+a^2x^2}}\dx{x}\text{,}\)
    Substitution: \(u=ax\)
  7. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{1}{1+ax^2}\dx{x}\text{,}\)
    Substitution: \(u=\sqrt{a}x\)
  8. \(\displaystyle \int \frac{\ln(x) }{x}\dx{x}\)
    Substitution: \(u=\ln(x) \)
