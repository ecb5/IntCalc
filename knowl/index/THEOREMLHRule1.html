Theorem 12.4.1.15. L’Hôpital’s Rule, (First Special Case).

Suppose \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) are differentiable on an open interval containing \(a\) and that \(f(a)=g(a)=0\text{.}\) Suppose also that
\begin{align*} \limit{x}{a}{f^\prime(x)}=L \amp{}\amp{}\text{ and }\amp{}\amp{} \limit{x}{a}{g^\prime(x)}=M\ne0. \end{align*}
Then
\begin{equation*} \limit{x}{a}{\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}}\lhopeq\frac{L}{M}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Notation.
We will use the notation “\(A\lhopeq B\)” to indicate that \(A\) is equal to \(B\) because of L’Hôpital’s Rule.
