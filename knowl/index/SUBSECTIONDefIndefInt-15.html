Consider the Initial Value Problem
\begin{align} \dfdx{y}{t}=2t\amp{}\amp{} \text{ and }\amp{}\amp{} y(0)=5.\tag{4.4} \end{align}
We saw in Example 1.1.2.2 that
\begin{equation} y(t)= \int{}\dx{y}=\int 2t\dx{t} =t^2+C\text{.}\tag{4.5} \end{equation}
Moreover, from the initial condition we see that
\begin{equation*} y(0)=0^2+C=5 \end{equation*}
or \(C=5\text{.}\) Thus the solution of IVP (4.4) is
\begin{equation*} y(t)=t^2+5\text{.} \end{equation*}
