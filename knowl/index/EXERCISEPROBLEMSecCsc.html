Problem 2.12.

We’ve integrated the \(\sin(x) \text{,}\) \(\cos(x) \text{,}\) \(\tan(x) \text{,}\) and \(\cot(x) \text{.}\) We will now address the integral of the \(\sec(x)\text{,}\) and \(\csc(x)\text{.}\)

(a)

Consider the following trick.
\begin{equation*} \int\sec(x)\dx{x} = \int\sec(x)\underbrace{\frac{\tan(x) +\sec(x) }{\tan(x) +\sec(x)}}_{=1}\dx{x}. \end{equation*}
Do you recognize a substitution that can be used? Use this to compute \(\int\sec(x)\dx{x}\text{.}\)

(b)

Use a similar trick to compute \(\int \csc(x) \dx{x} \text{.}\)
