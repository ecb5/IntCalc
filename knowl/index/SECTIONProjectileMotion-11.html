On the other hand, if we multiply both sides of equation (2.16) by \(\dx{t} \) seconds we get
\begin{equation*} \dx{v} \frac{\text{meters} }{\text{second} }=9.8\frac{\text{meters}}{\text{second}\cdot{}\cancel{\text{second}}}\cdot \dx{t}\cancel{\text{ seconds}}= 9.8\dx{t}\frac{\text{meters}}{\text{second}} \end{equation*}
In order to get a velocity by integration (summation) each of the terms being summed (the integrand) must be a velocity. Summing (integrating) both sides gives
\begin{equation*} \int\dx{v} \frac{\text{meters} }{\text{second} }=\int 9.8\dx{t}\frac{\text{meters}}{\text{second}} \end{equation*}
