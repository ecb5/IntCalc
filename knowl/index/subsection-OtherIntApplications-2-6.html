Huygens then developed a pendulum clock which would do this. He published his work in 1673 in his book Horologium Oscillatorium: sive de motu pendulorum ad horologia aptato demostrationes geometricae (The Pendulum Clock: or geometrical demonstrations concerning the motion of pendula as applied to clocks). The design from that work 11 . is seen below. To get the pendulum to swing along a cycloidal path, Huygen’s proved that if a flexible pendulum wraps around two flaps shaped like arches of a cycloid, then the bottom of the pendulum will trace a cycloid itself as seen on the left. This curve traced out is called the Involute of the cycloid 12 .
