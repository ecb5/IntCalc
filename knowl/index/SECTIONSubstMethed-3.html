In general, the faster the body is moving through a fluid (like air or water) the more the fluid tends to resist the motion. Hence the resistive force, or drag, is most simply modeled as a function of the body’s velocity. You can experience this directly by holding your hand out a car window. The faster the car moves, the more force you will feel on your hand. Similarly if you are swimming, the harder you pump your hands and feet the more force you will feel resisting the motion.
