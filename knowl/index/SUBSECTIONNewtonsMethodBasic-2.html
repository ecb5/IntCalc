We will use Newton’s Method to “re-invent” the Babylonian square root algorithm and, more importantly, to generalize it. Here’s the essential idea: We know how to find the root of a linear equation like \(ax+b=0\text{.}\) We want to use this knowledge to approximate the root of a nonlinear equation. As with the rest of Calculus this means that the Principle of Local Linearity 4.2.0.5 is the postulate underlying Newton’s Method.
