Drill 23.5.0.2. The Volume of a Square Based Pyramid.

(a)

Use the same idea as above to show that the volume of a square based pyramid is \(1/3\) the volume of the box with the same base and height

(b)

The Moscow Mathematical Papyrus (circa. 1850 BC) provided a formula for the volume of a truncated square based pyramid (called a frustum of a pyramid). Written in modern terms we have that volume of the following truncated pyramid
is given by
\begin{equation} V=\frac{h}{3}\left(a^2+ab+b^2\right).\tag{23.1} \end{equation}
The papyrus does not indicate how this was obtained. Use calculus to verify this formula.

(c)

Verify equation (23.1) by computing the volume of the frustum and the difference between two pyramids.
Hint.
Begin by adding in the part of the pyramid that was cut off to make the frustum.
