Problem 21.3.0.6.

(a)

Show that
\begin{equation*} y=Ae^{\sqrt{\frac{w}{h}}x}+Be^{-\sqrt{\frac{w}{h}}x} \end{equation*}
satisfies the above differential equation.

(b)

Show that if \(y(0)=\sqrt{\frac{h}{w}}\) and \(\dfdxat{y}{x}{0}=0\text{,}\) then \(A=B=\frac12{}\sqrt{\frac{h}{w}}\text{.}\) Compare this to the equation of the catenary in Problem in Context #53 in Differential Calculus.
