Integration gives us a more systematic way of solving differential equations. Suppose we need to evaluate the integral
\begin{equation*} \int x-e^x\dx{x} \text{.} \end{equation*}
Then we need to find an antiderivative of \(f(x)=x-e^x\text{.}\) An antiderivative of \(x\) is \(\frac{x^2}{2}\) and an antiderivative of \(e^x\) is \(e^x\text{,}\) so an antiderivative of \(x-e^x\) is \(\frac{x^2}{2}-e^x\text{.}\) Therefore
\begin{equation*} \int x-e^x\dx{x} = \frac{x^2}{2} - e^x +C \end{equation*}
(where \(C\) is an unspecified constant) because \(frac{x^2}{2} - e^x+C\) is the most general antiderivative of \(f(x)=x-e^x\text{.}\)
