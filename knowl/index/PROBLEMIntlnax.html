Problem 19.3.0.4.

Integrating by making a substitution seems to be a fairly simple technique when you first encounter it. But just as with differentiation it will turn out to be a very sophisticated technique. We will explore this a bit here.

(a)

Show that \(\int\ln(x)\dx{x} = x\ln(x) -x + c\)
Hint.
You don’t know how to compute this integral yet. Find another way.

(b)

Use the result of part 19.3.0.4.a to guess the value of
\begin{equation*} \int \ln(ax)\dx{x}\text{.} \end{equation*}

(c)

Now use the formula from part 19.3.0.4.a and an appropriate substitution to compute
\begin{equation*} \int \ln(ax)\dx{x}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Was your guess in Task 19.3.0.4.b correct

(d)

Differentiate the function you found in part 19.3.0.4.c to confirm that your solution is correct.
