Problem 19.2.0.4.

Integration by substitution seems to be a fairly simple technique when you first encounter it. But it will turn out to be a very sophisticated technique. We will explore this a bit here.

(a)

Verify that the identity \(\int\ln(x)\dx{x} = x\ln(x) -x + C\) is correct by differentiating.

(b)

Recall that \(\ln(ab)=\ln(a)+\ln(b)\text{.}\) Use this and the result of part 19.2.0.4.a to compute the value of
\begin{equation*} \int \ln(ax)\dx{x}\text{.} \end{equation*}

(c)

Now use the formula from part 19.2.0.4.a and the substitution \(u=ax\) to compute
\begin{equation*} \int \ln(ax)\dx{x} \end{equation*}
where \(a\) is an arbitrary constant. Was your result the same as in part 19.2.0.4.b?

(d)

Differentiate the function you found in part 19.2.0.4.c to confirm that your solution is correct.
