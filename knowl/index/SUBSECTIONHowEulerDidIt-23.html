From part (a) of Problem 8.13.1.6 we get the formula,
\begin{equation} e^{it}=\cos(t)+ i\sin(t).\tag{8.39} \end{equation}
equation (8.39) is known as Euler’s Identity and it is foundational to electrical engineering, and physics (especially Quantum Mechanics). For mathematicians it is the key to understanding the geometry of the Complex Numbers (numbers of the form \(a+bi\text{,}\) where \(a\) and \(b\) are real numbers and \(i^2=-1\text{.}\)
