Definition 20.1.3.1. Indefinite Integral.

Given \(f(x)\) the formula
\begin{equation*} \int f(x)\dx{x} \end{equation*}
always represents the multifunction whose derivative is \(f(x)\text{.}\)
For simplicity, and because it has become standard, we will almost always refer \(\int f(x)\dx{x}\) as the indefinite integral of \(f(x)\text{,}\) rather than as a multifunction.
Because \(f(x)\) and \(\int f(x)\dx{x}\) are so closely related it is common to define
\begin{equation*} F(x)=\int f(x)\dx{x} \end{equation*}
and call \(F(x)\) the indefinite integral of \(f(x)\text{,}\) just because it is less to write.
