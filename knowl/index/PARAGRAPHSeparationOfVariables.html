Separation of Variables.

The solution technique on display in Problem 20.1.0.2 is called separation of variables and it is quite general. Essentially, we can solve a differential equation whenever we can first “separate” the variables (say \(y\) and \(x\)) into the form
\begin{equation} g(y)\dx{y}=f(x)\dx{x}\text{.}\tag{20.7} \end{equation}
Next we sum the differentials, giving
\begin{equation*} \int{}g(y)\dx{y}=\int{}f(x)\dx{x}\text{.} \end{equation*}
If we are able to evaluate each of the integrals \(\int{}g(y)\dx{y}\) and \(\int{}f(x)\dx{x}\) then we can solve equation (20.7).
