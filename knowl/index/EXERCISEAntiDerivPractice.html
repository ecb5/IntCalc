Problem 1.5.

Show that the following statements are true.

(a)

\(\int 2te^{t^2}\dx{t} = e^{t^2} +C\)

(b)

\(\int te^t\dx{t} = (t-1)e^t+C\)

(c)

\(\int \sin(x)\cos(x)\dx{x} =\frac12\sin^2(x)+C\)

(d)

\(\int \sin(x)\cos(x)\dx{x} = -\frac12\cos^2(x)+C\)
Notice that we’ve used \(x\) instead of \(t\) for our variable. Does that matter? Explain.

(e)

\(\int u\cos(u) \dx{u} = u\sin(u)+\cos(u)+C\)

(f)

\(\int x\ln(x) \dx{x} = x(\ln{x}-1)+C\)
