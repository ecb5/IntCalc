We assume, with Leibniz, that equation (20.3) remains true when the finite differences (\(\Delta{x}\)) are replaced with infinitesimal differences (\(\dx{x}{}\)):
\begin{equation} \int{\dx{x} }=x_n-x_0=x\text{.}\tag{20.4} \end{equation}
The symbol “\(\int \)” is called an integral sign, and any expression starting with “\(\int \)” followed by a differential is called an integral.
