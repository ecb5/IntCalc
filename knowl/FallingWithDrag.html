Problem 1.28.

(a)

Use Newton's Third Law of Motion: (\(F\))\(=\)(\(M\))ass\(\cdot{}\)(\(A\))cceleration, to show that the velocity of the ball must satisfy the equations:
\begin{equation*} M\dfdx{v}{t}=Mg-Rv^2, \hskip5mm{}v(0)=0\text{.} \end{equation*}
Show that the IVP above is equivalent to
\begin{equation*} \frac{\dx{v}}{1-\frac{R}{Mg}v^2}=g\dx{t}, \hskip5mm v(0)=0\text{.} \end{equation*}

(b)

Use the substitution \(\sqrt{\frac{R}{Mg}}v = \sin(\theta)\) to show that
\begin{equation*} v=\sqrt{\frac{Mg}{R}}\left[\frac{1-e^{-2gt}}{1+e^{-2gt}}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}

(c)

Compute \(\limit{t}{\infty}{v}\text{.}\) Compare this terminal velocity with our previous results.
